Decor

The first thing I notice
as I make my way down the hall
is a peculiar decor of the wall
across from her bedroom door.
There’s a tiny bud of lotus
that, according to all the lore,

should promise a hopeful future
all the way down the road.
No fucks given about the code,
I stare into the crow’s eyes
that follow me like a tutor
in a boarding school late at night.

When I get to the kitchen, I linger
for a bit, looking at the cheap art
in a frame. It makes my heart
sick as I think of what brought her
to get a cheesy painting by a singer,
then I get my glass of water.

She’s in her bed, quietly snoring.
I’ll be surely gone, come morning.

Of your spirit

Show me your true colours, darling,
and your love.

I won't notice your dress, scarlet,
delicate; above
the carnival of flesh has started
in a small flat
That only holds three sparkling
people, and the rest
are in the dirty hallway.
We go a-knocking
to the door that has seen better day
and lazy mornings.
There we enter the festival
of kind hands,
they embrace us in caramel
of their lands.
Love, consider it medical
for a night.
I'm becoming less lexical,
it's a fight.

We are dancing in ritual
and in flames.
Darling, pass me the pitcher of
bubbles' prance.

You are hiding under your
coldest face.
Show me your true colours, baby,
and your dance.

She loves me (not)

She loves me
while texting at three am
laughing softly at each side of the line
not to wake our parents up.

She loves me not
while fighting about something
that hurts us both and leaves
only when the morning comes.

She loves me
when she looks up from my embrace
and sees my sleepy smile,
smiling in return.

She loves me not
when it’s different outside
and I can’t talk to anyone,
locking myself in my head.

She loves me
when we cook together,
bumping our hips slightly in the kitchen
and giggling between kisses.

She loves me not
when we speak too much
and it makes all that’s real
seem so fake.

She loves me
when she hugs me
after a severe panic attack
and tells my shaking hands so.

She loves me not
when I don’t ask her what’s wrong
when clearly something is,
and she feels empty.

She loves me.

Rest, my love

Rest, my love. Rest
your heavy head on my thighs,
let your hair stretch to the west
from your dry and weary eyes,
falling in a waterfall of insanity.
Let me brush and calm
them, suspend your fear of gravity
and lead you by the palm
to the realm of serenity.

I will not contest your passion,
but give you my remedy,
should thou wish so. Ashes
of your demons stain my hands.
Words burn my tongue: I shall not
speak them. My body's lands
are open to your embrace. A sought
after by all existence love
covers you as snow.
It will keep you safe; above
the Way with a milky flow
passes by my cheek to soothe you.
My fingers weave your hair into
the thread of life, disentangling
your pains and evicting them.


My lips touch your soft smile,
catching on the gentleness of your state,
half-awake, yet eager to kiss a while,
the touches that sweeten and placate
your very soul.

As you melt, I kiss your forehead
and let your hair stream down my legs.
My lips find the way to the florid
condensed sunlight in them, which begs
to be caressed. Contented, you thaw
under my touch and stay deep
in me and quietness, lest
It wakes you up. I watch in awe;
you're on my thighs; you fall asleep.

Rest, my love. Rest.

Palmistry

I knew a girl with warm hands
and a dull soul
who could never be enough.
She looked at me with kind eyes
and a soft smile.
She wanted to make
all the voices quiet
but never understood what they stood for.

I knew a girl with cold hands
and the brightest gaze.
She wanted to become as mad as me
without ever knowing the struggle.
She wanted to drive me insane,
but all it did
was drive me away.

I knew a girl with soft hands
whose nails were always painted beige
or gray
or white
or some other nondescript color.
She never understood me,
but she kissed like she did.
And loved me for being a mystery.

I knew a girl with stained hands
that always bore the brand of ink
from all the stuff she had to write down
and learn by heart.
She seemed to love me despite my madness
but tried to never show it.
And she would say so much
that she said nothing.

I know a girl with pretty hands
that weave into my hair
and make me smile.
She knows all the lyrics
to my favorite song,
but hasn’t ever heard the voices.
She likes me fine
and is willing to work
to stay with me.

I will know a girl with kind hands
who’s learnt every voice by heart
and knows what they sound like.
She will have seen all of my favorite shows,
and some of them with me.
She will be cruelly kind when I need it
and tell me the truth.
She will love me for who I am
and like my voices fine.
She will stay because she wants to
and it will be over
before it begins.

Once

I once knew a girl.

She was always sweet, she wore classic cherry chapstick
and had low heels on her feet.
She wore a cross and laughed gently,
frowned upon sex before marriage and lived across
from a dilapidated ice-cream truck
that was abandoned in a parking lot.
As time went on, I almost forgot about her,
and about the way she was purged
of half of her lunch and all of her dinner
every night of the week.
She wished me to fare well.
I saw her only on my way to hell.

I once knew a boy.

He was often quiet, thoughtful and almost kind.
The riot of his house was to
leave the front door unlocked when getting groceries.
He loved folklore and, to be honest, any
kind of research. He only had good intentions
and he was caring. So much was
scaring his parents that he never went out alone.
I saw scars that adorned his wrist
and knew him as one of us.
His face was the only one not laughing
at me as I fell into the vortex.

I once was someone.

Though I can’t remember who.
I felt this morning like I could be new,
but failed. In the light of a sun
I touched my skin that bailed from a city flat
that used to belong
to someone who was me at the time.
They were pretty and nice and had bright hair, blue or green
sometimes.
They hated telephone calls
and loved to walk in
the rain.
They wanted a
cat and a
frog.
They were and
then
they weren’t.
I heard they either hung themselves
or slit their wrists
or maybe OD’ed.
I never
knew for
sure.
I knew
I couldn’t
find
a
cure.

I am one with the tide

I am one with the tide.

Older than day, my soul rises above time. Above the ticking and tocking clocks, counting seconds and hours, tracking the time left to those concerned; above minutes and ages, it rises. I do not feel the changing of eras and epochs; I am infinite.

Wider than my body, my soul takes up no space. Wider, wider; bigger than countries, planets, and galaxies, it sprinkles stardust in a swooping motion to create life anew. It is of no space at all, it seeps into everything and everyone around me, rounding them up and righting wrongs with gleeful raw love. It is unstoppable.

It is older than any prayer, older than any crucifix pointing up or down hanged above a sick one’s bed. It is older than up or down, older than any cry for help, older than words, older than cruelty and charity.

It blends itself into the river’s bend, into a bottle that would rather hold a ship than wine; be the product rather than a facilitator of dreams and delicious delusion. Older than devastation of the land, than delirious whisperings of those who have seen the light, older than light itself.

It runs with the fastest among us, with those who need to catch and gather; it can be heard in their screams needed for survival, fuelled by adrenaline, uninhibited. And it flows through a lover’s whisper; it is felt in fingers carding through hair.

It is all the gentleness and all the sacrifice, rolled into one.

I am as old as the night, much older than the moon that guides the waters. I am bigger than the milky way, whose movement I feel deeply grounded in my solar plexus. I feel all the molecules that compose the earth, that compose the stars, and those that have broken or are yet to form. I feel on behalf of every being, of every atom, and everything in existence.

I am infinite.
I am one with the tide.
